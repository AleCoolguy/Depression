Stages:

Depression often manifests in various stages, each with distinct emotional and physical symptoms. Initially, individuals may experience mild feelings of sadness or disinterest, which can escalate into persistent low mood and fatigue. As the condition progresses, symptoms may intensify, leading to significant changes in appetite, sleep disturbances, and feelings of hopelessness or worthlessness. In the more severe stages, individuals may struggle with daily functioning, experiencing social withdrawal and a lack of motivation. Ultimately, untreated depression can culminate in thoughts of self-harm or suicide, highlighting the importance of early intervention and support. Understanding these stages can aid in recognizing the need for professional help and fostering a compassionate approach to those affected.
